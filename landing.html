Most networks are built on proximity, not alignment.

LinkedIn connects you to people in your industry. That's not the same as people who think like you. Proximity is a weak signal — you end up mapped to colleagues, competitors, and conference contacts instead of the people who are actually working on the same problems.

The people who will change your trajectory are usually outside your field. Authors writing about the same questions from a different angle. Researchers with ten years of depth in a domain you're just entering. Practitioners who've already failed at what you're about to try.

ORBIT finds them by reading who you are, not what you do.

How it works

  1. 01 Fill out your gravity profile. Your mission, worldview, and thinking style. This is your signal — everything ORBIT finds flows from it.
  2. 02 Get suggested people based on your specific profile. Real authors, researchers, and practitioners — confirmed by web search, with contact info and a specific reason why they belong in your orbit.
  3. 03 Track relationships across 4 stages. From identified to inner circle. Search for anyone specific. Build deliberately, not by accident.

Search by story, not by industry.

ORBIT uses your gravity profile as a lens. Two people can have the same job title and belong in completely different orbits. The tool finds the signal in who you are — your exact domain terms, your specific questions, the problems you've named — not the category you fit into.

Every suggestion is grounded in a live web search. ORBIT extracts the precise terms from your profile and searches for people active in those exact spaces. No database. No pre-built lists. Just your gravity, translated into names.
